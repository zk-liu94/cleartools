Using Word Frequency to Improve Your English Writing

June 24, 2026

One of the best ways to improve your English writing is to understand which words you use most often. Word frequency analysis shows you exactly this. By knowing your most common words, you can identify overused vocabulary and work on expanding your word choices.

What Is Word Frequency Analysis?

Word frequency analysis counts how many times each word appears in your text. The words that appear most often are shown at the top. This gives you a clear picture of your writing habits.

How to Use Our Word Frequency Counter

Try our Word Frequency Counter to analyze your writing. Simply paste your English text into the tool and it will show your most commonly used words in a visual chart. You can use it on blog posts, emails, essays, or any English writing.

What to Look For

  • Overused transition words: If "however", "therefore", and "moreover" appear too often, try varying your transitions.
  • Weak adjectives: Words like "good", "bad", "big", "small" can often be replaced with more specific alternatives.
  • Filler words: "Very", "really", "actually" — these add little meaning.
  • Limited verbs: If you use "said", "went", "got" repeatedly, find more precise verbs.

Tips for Expanding Your Vocabulary

Once you know which words you overuse, you can work on alternatives. For example, instead of always using "good", try "excellent", "helpful", "effective", or "valuable" depending on the context.

Use a thesaurus to find alternatives, but always check that the new word fits naturally. The word counter and readability checker can help you track your progress over time.
